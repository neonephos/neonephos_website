# Proposing a New Project

NeoNephos always welcomes new open source projects which share the overarching goal of providing an open access multi-provider cloud-edge continuum and strengthening European digital cloud sovereignty.

This document highlights how NeoNephos benefits your project and how to best prepare for a successful project's submission.

## What NeoNephos does for You

### A Vendor Neutral Home for the Project and Assets

All projects with their respective trademarks are owned by Linux Foundation Europe (LFEU) - a reputable non-profit. The NeoNephos agenda is driven by the [Governing Board](governing_board.md) which is
open to any organization willing to become a member. The technical leadership is facilitated by the [Technical Advisory Council (TAC)](technical_advisory_council.md), which is not just open
to company representatives but also the technical leaders of the respective projects which could be anyone.

This setup allows multiple companies and individuals to collaborate in a mutually beneficial arrangement. In no way is a single organization privileged on any of the boards. All important decisions are done through voting.
And having the Linux Foundation Europe be the ultimate owner behind the assets and trademarks ensures neutrality.

### Increased Exposure

NeoNephos features projects on its [website and media](https://neonephos.org). In addition, advanced projects may be featured in press releases, events or blog posts.
Projects benefit from the combined outreach capabilities of NeoNephos and LFEU to ensure they are introduced to the desired audience.

### Access to Foundational Support

It is the goal of the foundation to see its projects succeed. Therefore, advanced projects can request foundational resources like
infrastructure, expertise or funding to ensure there are no logistical or financial constraints impeding progress.

### Shape European Digital Risiliency 

Have a front seat in shaping Europe's digital resilience of tomorrow. Interact with key players in the industry to advance our shared agenda.

## Considerations for the TAC

When you hand in a proposal for a new project, the [TAC](https://github.com/neonephos/tac) considers multiple things, such as:

- Does the project address an important issue that aligns with NeoNephos' objectives?
- Is there active or at least potential for industry adoption?
- Can NeoNephos satisfy the resource demands of the new project?
- Does the project lend itself well to open source collaboration?
- Are there any legal issues with the project?
- Is the project leadership a cultural fit?

Thinking about how to meet these questions early on increases the probability of a successful application.

## Preparing Your Application

<img src="/public/project_day0.svg" alt="Project Operations Day 1" width="500"/>

You should first read and understand the NeoNephos Project Lifecycle document carefully. The application process can then be simplified to:

- [Find 2 TAC members](technical_advisory_council.md) supporting the proposal
- Together with the 2 TAC members address each stage requirement

Keep in mind that the stage suitable for most projects is Incubation, but your TAC supporters will help you choose a stage if in doubt.

## Fill out the New Project Issue Template

<img src="/public/project_proposal.gif" alt="Project Proposal GitHub Issue Tracker Animation" width="700"/>

The submission is done on [GitHub via issue templates](https://github.com/neonephos/community/issues/new?template=new_project_proposal.yaml). Answer all the questions to the best of your ability and create the corresponding GitHub issue. All further discussions about the project proposal then should happen on the GitHub issue as comments.

## Code Scan

After you submitted your application, LFEU staff will conduct a code license and project name scan to check for any issues.
To ease this step it is best practice to lay out the code licenses in a machine friendly way (e.g. [REUSE](https://reuse.software/) format).
Monitor the status of your GitHub issue to be informed about any questions or updates. 

## TAC Presentation

Next, you will present your project at a TAC meeting where it will be voted upon. In the future, we may provide a slides template to structure your presentation.

## Trademark Handover

After approval, your TAC supporters and LFEU staff will support you in transferring your trademarks and assets for the benefit of the Linux Foundation Europe.
It is best to prepare a checklist of all GitHub repository logins, social media credentials, code and other assets to accelerate this step.

## Done

Congratulations, you are done with transferring your project to NeoNephos. But the journey is far from over. After approval, you'll receive further instructions on the things to do on day 1.

**Propose new projects [here](https://github.com/neonephos/community/issues/new?template=new_project_proposal.yaml).**
